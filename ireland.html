Education system in Ireland

Study levels in Ireland

The education system in Ireland encompasses four primary levels, each serving a distinct purpose in shaping the academic and professional trajectories of its students. These levels include primary education, which lays the foundation for learning; secondary education, where students delve deeper into various subjects and prepare for further studies or entry into the workforce; further education, providing specialized training and skills development for specific careers or interests; and higher education, offering undergraduate and postgraduate degrees across a wide range of disciplines. While bearing some similarities to the UK system, Ireland's education framework also reflects its unique cultural and historical influences, fostering a rich and diverse learning environment.

  • Primary education including pre-primary
  • Post primary education
  • Further education and training
  • Higher education

Know all about the intakes available in the Ireland

In Ireland, universities and colleges typically offer two main intakes for various programs: the autumn intake, which begins in September or October, and the spring intake, commencing in January or February. The autumn intake serves as the primary entry point for most undergraduate and postgraduate programs, aligning with the start of the academic year. Application processes for autumn intake generally open well in advance, often the year before, with varying deadlines depending on the institution and program. Conversely, the spring intake provides an alternative opportunity, particularly for select postgraduate programs, allowing students to start their studies in the spring semester. Application deadlines for spring intake tend to fall earlier in the year, necessitating timely preparation. Prospective students should consult their chosen institutions for specific intake dates and application deadlines tailored to their desired programs.

3. Is IELTS required to work in Ireland?

Minimum IELTS Score required to study in Ireland is 6.5 overall and no individual band below 6

4. What are the requirements to study in Ireland?

Admission requirements in Ireland differ based on the university and chosen major. However, international students applying to universities in Ireland usually need to submit the following documents:

University course or degree transcripts

English language test results, such as IELTS

Work experience and samples (if required)

GMAT or GRE scores

Statement of purpose

Recommendation letter

5. What are the requirements for Ireland student visa?

Here are the requirements for a student visa:

Fill out the application form.

Provide an application letter.

Include two passport-sized photographs taken in the last 6 months.

Submit your current passport and copies of previous passports.

Present evidence of enrolment in the course.

Provide an explanation for any gaps in your educational history.

6. Can I study in Ireland backlog?

Elite Overseas typically consider applications with a maximum of four to five backlogs, although some may accept a maximum of two to three backlogs.
