Part time job and Other details

Part-Time Job

In Sweden, international students are permitted to work while studying, with no official limitation on the number of hours allowed, although studies should take priority. Universities provide support to students seeking part-time employment, offering help desks to assist in job searches. Additionally, universities offer guidance on preparing CVs and handling interviews, often organizing job fairs to facilitate connections between students and potential employers.


Stay Back Options

In Sweden, students must receive an offer of employment and apply for a work permit before their study permit expires, as no additional time is provided for the application process. If students meet certain conditions, they may be eligible to receive the work permit and work legally in Sweden.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which degrees can a student study for in Sweden?

Swedish universities offer bachelor’s, master’s, and PhD degrees.

When can a student apply for Master’s programs starting in autumn 2014?

The application period Master’s programs starting autumn 2014 is 17th October. The application will be open until 15th January.

Are there tuition fees at Swedish universities?

Yes Universities set their own tuition fees, and these vary between SEK 80,000–140,000 per academic year.

How many programs can a student apply for?

Can apply for up to 4 programs at Master’s level via www.universityadmissions.se and must rank them in order of preference.

Can a student apply for a scholarship for studying in Sweden?

The Swedish Institute offers some scholarships to postgraduate studies. Universities offer a range of different scholarship programmes for international students to help cover tuition fees or living costs.

Can a student send a copy of TOEFL results?

No, all TOEFL tests must be sent straight from Educational Testing Service (ETS) and reach University Admissions in Sweden by the deadline for submitting documents.

When is the deadline for receipt of supporting academic documents?

Deadline for receipt of supporting academic documents is 1 February 2014.
