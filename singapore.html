Why study in Singapore ?

Singapore is rapidly establishing itself as a formidable force, propelled by government initiatives prioritizing education. Renowned for its excellence in education, Singapore was ranked among QS's top three "Best Student Cities" in 2013. This reputation has led to increasing demand for Singaporean graduates by employers, not only in Asia but also globally, highlighting the country's influence and prominence in the field of education and its impact on the global workforce.

Singapore is bustling with life from dawn till dusk, offering a vibrant array of activities and events that continue well into the evening. The city boasts an exceptional variety of local cuisine, ranging from traditional delicacies to international flavors, satisfying every palate. With unparalleled shopping experiences and world-class attractions such as the celebrated zoo, Singapore ensures that there is never a dull moment for residents and visitors alike, promising endless opportunities for exploration and enjoyment.

Education System

English serves as the medium of instruction in Singapore's education system, yet one of its distinctive competitive advantages lies in its bilingual policy. The nation's government and educational institutions provide a range of scholarships for international students, facilitating access to higher education opportunities. Moreover, Singaporean universities, in collaboration with institutions from other countries, organize various exchange programs, enriching students' academic experiences and cultural perspectives. While the cost of education in Singapore is relatively low compared to other study abroad destinations, financial assistance is available through government schemes and university scholarships and fellowships, making education in Singapore accessible and affordable for students from diverse backgrounds.

Higher education is offered in 2 types of Universities

1. National Universities:

Singapore is home to six national universities, each offering a diverse range of undergraduate and postgraduate degree programs, including doctoral degrees. These institutions are the National University of Singapore, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore Management University, Singapore University of Technology & Design, Singapore Institute of Technology, and SIM University. Together, they provide students with a wealth of educational opportunities across various fields of study, contributing to Singapore's reputation as a leading hub for higher education and research.

2. Private universities:

In addition to its national universities, Singapore hosts several foreign universities that have established campuses in the country, offering a variety of undergraduate and postgraduate degree programs. Some of these institutions include the Chicago Booth School of Business, Technische Universität München, James Cook University Singapore, University of Adelaide, Southern Cross University, University of New Brunswick, Queen Margaret University, Temple University, City University of New York, Baruch College, University of Nevada, Las Vegas, Aventis School of Management, and Curtin University of Technology. This diversity of educational offerings reflects Singapore's status as a global education hub, attracting students from around the world to pursue their academic aspirations in a dynamic and multicultural environment.

Part time job and Other details

Part-Time Job

Certain universities in Singapore allow students to work part-time for up to 16 hours per week without requiring a work permit. This arrangement provides students with the opportunity to gain work experience and supplement their income while pursuing their studies.


Stay Back Options

After completing their degree program, students in Singapore should submit an application for a one-year Visit Pass (Long-Term) to seek employment in the country. Employers are responsible for making this application on behalf of the students. Additionally, students enrolled at national universities typically sign a 3-year bond in exchange for subsidized tuition fees. This bond serves as a commitment to contribute to Singapore's workforce for a specified period after graduation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is education in Singapore Free ?

The education in Singapore is quite low as compared to other study abroad destinations like UK, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, etc. Students have option to pay subsidised tuition fee with an obligation to work for 3 years in Singapore under the Tuition Grant Bond.

Are Degree program taught in English ?

Yes the medium of instruction is English

Are IELTS / TOEFL Required ?

Yes Singapore institutions need IELTS or equivalent as a proof of English proficiency. Polytechnic Diploma & Bachelor programs require overall band score of 6.0 or equivalent. Master Degree requires IELTS 6.0 – 6.5 or equivalent depending on the program.

How long do Singapore master degree take?

Master program duration can range from 1-2 years full-time and the curriculum can be more or less structured depending on the institution. MBA programmes can take between 12-18 months full-time. Part-time and full-time options are available for most Masters Programmes.

What is the average tuition fee ?
