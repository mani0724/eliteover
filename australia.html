Why study in Australia ?

Australia has emerged as the third most sought-after destination for international students within the English-speaking realm. Despite having a modest number of universities, Australia provides an education of global renown, frequently ranking prominently in international assessments. In the latest QS World University Rankings, eight Australian universities secured positions within the top 100. Moreover, Australia boasts one of the highest standards of living globally. International students have the opportunity to work part-time while pursuing their studies, thus mitigating living expenses. Additionally, scholarships are available, offering financial assistance and making studying in Australia more accessible for international students.

  • Courses are recognized internationally, Graduates from Australia are very successful in finding jobs and hold prominent positions worldwide.
  • A dynamic, vibrant country and its people are energetic, friendly and confident. Multicultural Australia is a safe, friendly, sophisticated.
  • Education standards are higher and the academic environment is stimulating, It offers an education experience that makes a real difference.
  • Most courses and training undertaken by international students in Australia are covered by Australian Qualification Framework (AQF). It is a system of learning which is authorised by the Australian government.
  • Living costs and tuition fees are comparatively lower than most other countries.
  • Allowed to work 20 hours per week. to earn money, gain useful work experience in a foreign country and meet a variety of new people.
  • High Visa success rate.

Education System

The Australian education system is structured into three main sectors: University, Vocational Education and Training (VET), and English Language.

1. Indeed, the University sector represents the pinnacle of education in Australia. Students have the opportunity to pursue undergraduate studies, leading to a Bachelor's degree, or postgraduate studies, encompassing Graduate Certificates, Diplomas, Masters, and PhD programs.

University of Applied Science:

2.Vocational institutions in Australia are strongly connected to industry, offering courses that are highly practical and skill-based. These Vocational Education and Training (VET) courses are available at government-funded TAFE (Technical and Further Education) institutes as well as private institutions. Many VET courses offer pathways to further education by providing advanced standing or credit transfer into university degree programs.

3.English language courses in Australia cater to various purposes such as study, travel, immigration, or business. Approximately 100 private English language centers operate across the country, providing a wide range of courses. Additionally, some universities and TAFE institutes offer English language programs, allowing students to enhance their language skills while pursuing other academic or vocational studies.

Part time job and Other details

Part-Time Job

In Australia, students must obtain a student visa with work permission to be eligible for employment while studying. Securing both the visa and work permission concurrently streamlines the process, saving time and expenses. Upon commencing classes, students can begin working immediately. Under a student visa, individuals are permitted to work up to 40 hours per fortnight during the term and unrestricted hours during scheduled course breaks, providing flexibility to manage their studies and employment.


Stay Back Options

Australia maintains a steady demand for foreign workers, particularly for graduates who have completed their degrees in the country. Special work visa categories exist for such students, facilitating their transition into the workforce. Additionally, Australia offers several other visa categories accessible to international students. Moreover, the country provides a clear pathway to permanent residency through a points-based system, offering opportunities for long-term settlement.

Institutions in Australia rigorously assess whether students meet the selection criteria outlined for their intended course of study. This evaluation involves scrutinizing the level and content of the education the student has completed in their home country. By considering factors such as academic qualifications, relevant experience, and alignment with the proposed course, institutions ensure that students possess the necessary foundation to succeed in their chosen field of study.

Undergraduate

In Australia, undergraduate courses typically necessitate proof of completing an Australian Senior Secondary Certificate of Education (Year 12) or its international equivalent, such as obtaining three passes at GCE A Levels in appropriate subjects. Additionally, specific prerequisite subjects may be required for certain programs. For postgraduate courses, applicants are generally expected to have successfully completed an undergraduate degree. However, confirmed research ability or relevant work experience may also be taken into account during the assessment process. Overall, institutions meticulously evaluate candidates based on their academic background, relevant experience, and potential to excel in the chosen field of study.

Student visas for Australia can be applied for through two methods: E-Visa (Electronic Visa) and Paper Visa. For E-Visa applications, students are required to pay the Visa Application fee using a Credit Card during the E-Lodgement process. Additionally, they may need to pay VFS charges. Conversely, for Paper Visa applications, students must pay both the Visa Application Fee and the VFS Charges. It's important for applicants to carefully follow the specified procedures and requirements for their chosen visa application method.

To apply for a student visa for Australia, students are required to submit a series of documents including Form 157A, Form 956, and Form 1282. Additionally, they need to provide a copy of their passport (including the biodata page, address page, and any observation page if applicable), four passport-sized photographs, and all educational documents along with their IELTS score card. Moreover, a declaration on affidavit from the student, documents related to employment (previous work experience or current employment details), a Statement of Purpose, and any other supporting documents relevant to the visa application process must be included in the submission. It's crucial for applicants to ensure all required documents are accurately prepared and submitted according to the specified guidelines to facilitate the visa application process.

Sponsors providing financial support for student visa applicants are required to submit several documents, including an Affidavit of Sponsorship from the sponsor, along with a valid photo ID proof such as an Election ID, Ration Card, PAN Card, or Driving License. Additionally, sponsors must provide Income Tax Returns (ITRs) for the last three years and evidence of their income. It's essential for students to demonstrate sufficient funds according to the program, subclass, and assessment level they have selected. This includes covering living expenses amounting to $18,610 per annum, Overseas Student Health Cover costing $ 478 per annum, and travel expenses estimated at 2,000. Adhering to these financial requirements is crucial for a successful student visa application process.

When applying for a student visa in Australia, students must demonstrate a sufficient level of English language proficiency and meet minimum academic requirements. Australian educational institutions are only permitted to admit students who possess an appropriate level of English proficiency. These institutions establish their own English language requirements, which may vary depending on the proposed level of the course. It's important to note that these requirements may differ from the English language proficiency levels required for the student visa application. Therefore, students should ensure they meet both the institution's English language requirements and the visa application requirements to avoid any complications during the application process.

In Australia, students have various options for accommodation, both on and off campus. The country offers a high standard of living arrangements, including halls of residence, boarding schools, homestays, and rented houses, apartments, or rooms. On-campus accommodation provides convenience and a supportive environment, often fostering a sense of community among students.

University accommodation : Most universities offer a variety of accommodation on or near to campus such as university apartments, residential colleges or halls of residence. Halls of Residence-cheaper and a popular option with full-time international students. Residential colleges-includes meals, library, computer access, sporting and social facilities. It is usually for full-time students and students are advised to apply early as these are usually high in demand.

Homestay: Students live with a local family during the course of study. Students are welcomed into the home and accepted as a member of the family, participating in all daily routines of the family. Inclusive of food and electricity. (Mon-Fri breakfast and dinner, Sat & Sun three meals)

Shared / Rental accommodation: Students often share with fellow students and may have to provide their own furniture. Students usually advertise for room wanted or a room available in newspapers and notice boards on campus.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cost of living ?

The cost of living in Australia can vary on an individual choice of city or town. A student in Australia will need $18,610 Per Annum. This amount covers rent, food, basic clothing, public transport & books.

What are Australian university semester dates?

Generally Australian universities have two semesters. The first is from February to June and the second from July to November. This can vary somewhat between universities.

How much does it cost to study in Australia?

Studying in Australia varies in cost depending on factors like institution, program, and location, but it's generally considered more expensive compared to many other countries due to the quality of education and living standards.

Do students need a tax file number if studying in Australia?

Australian Agency recommends that a student get an Australian tax file number (TFN). student will need this for banking and tax purposes if they are work during study. Every Australian Post Office will have an application form available. Or can apply online.
