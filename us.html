Why study in United States of America?

The United States of America stands as a global leader in education, boasting a significant number of top-ranked universities according to major international rankings. Few countries rival the USA in terms of the sheer number of prestigious institutions and esteemed academics, with nearly 50 of the top 200 universities in the world based in the country. This rich academic landscape has solidified the USA's reputation as a hub for excellence in higher education, attracting students and scholars from around the globe to pursue their academic and research aspirations.

  • World class learning institutions with Endless study choices and Offers wide range of Degrees with opportunity to change majors or opt for multiple specializations.
  • Worldwide recognition with advance scientific and technological research in production of innovative technological products.
  • US education system offers a kind of flexibility in terms of the courses that are offered, which enables to select the choice of subject from a variety of topics.
  • International students are allowed to work for up to 20 hours a week during term time and full-time during holiday.
  • Financial Support is available in most of universities like student grants, loans and stipends to cover daily expenses as well as tuition in some cases.
  • Land Of Opportunities -Job opportunities are more, when compared to other countries.

Education System

Types of Universities:

Public universities in the United States, which are government-affiliated institutions, offer undergraduate degree programs as well as graduate education, including Master's and Ph.D. degrees. These universities are typically funded and operated by state governments or other public entities, and they often prioritize accessibility and affordability for students in their respective states or regions. Public universities play a crucial role in higher education by providing a wide range of academic disciplines and research opportunities to students at both the undergraduate and graduate levels.

Private universities in the United States, unlike public institutions, are not government-affiliated and typically rely on tuition fees, donations, and endowments for funding. Despite their higher cost, they often offer smaller class sizes, personalized attention, and specialized programs, making them appealing to students seeking a high-quality education. Examples include Harvard University, Stanford University, and Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).

Part time job and Other details

Part-Time Job

On-campus jobs for international students in the United States include positions such as Teaching Assistants, Research Assistants, working in college libraries, or in admission offices. These roles offer valuable experience and may help cover living expenses while studying. Off-campus jobs typically require Curricular Practical Training (CPT) authorization, which is granted after nine months of academic study. CPT allows students to gain work experience directly related to their field of study, providing practical training opportunities beyond the campus environment.


Stay Back Options

In the United States, international students from STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics) backgrounds are eligible to stay for up to 29 months after completing their studies, while those from non-STEM backgrounds are typically permitted to stay for 12 months. This extended period for STEM students allows them more time to gain practical experience through Optional Practical Training (OPT) in their field of study, contributing to their professional development and potentially leading to employment opportunities in the country.
