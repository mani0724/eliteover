Why study in Canada?

Canada stands out as one of the premier international study destinations for numerous compelling reasons. Its educational landscape offers a diverse array of course options, spanning from undergraduate diplomas to master's degrees, catering to a wide range of academic interests and career aspirations. Moreover, research is a cornerstone of Canadian universities, positioning the country at the forefront of technological advancements and innovation. Renowned for its peaceful and safe environment, Canada consistently ranks among the best countries to live in globally. Notably, three Canadian cities—Vancouver, Toronto, and Calgary—are recognized among the top 10 "Most Liveable Cities" worldwide, further enhancing Canada's appeal as an exceptional destination for international students.

  • Canada’s universities are amongst the top international ranking universities and maintain high standards of academic excellence with tuition fees being the lowest in English-speaking countries.
  • The United Nations ranked Canada as one of the best places in the world to live. Students in Canada opt to work after graduation and make Canada permanent home by applying for permanent residency
  • Very multicultural over 90 languages spoken in larger cities.
  • Canadian Universities are diversified and have good quality with variety of programs (certificates, diplomas, associate degrees, degrees, masters, doctorate, etc.)
  • Canadian government have subsidized tuition fees are relatively affordable when compared to US, UK and Australian Universities. Canada provides a solid investment in education.
  • Land Of Opportunities -Job opportunities are more, when Canada has one of the world’s best education systems, with outstanding programs in virtually all fields and Canadian university degrees are highly valued by employers around the world.
  • Canada is increasing the number of International students so encouraging a lot of new students to apply at Canadian universities.

1. In Canada, education is governed by provincial jurisdictions, leading to significant variations in the education systems across different provinces. These differences encompass curriculum, assessment methods, and administrative structures, reflecting the diverse cultural and social landscapes. Despite these distinctions, the overarching aim of providing quality education remains a shared priority nationwide.

2.The education system in Canada encompasses

Publicly-funded universities:

Universities are generally Public funded, provides courses ranging from Degree to P.HD and research based.

Private community colleges, technical institutes, career colleges, universities, and university colleges in Canada offer diploma programs that are skill-based and job-oriented. These programs are designed to equip students with practical skills and knowledge relevant to specific industries or career paths. Additionally, many of these institutions facilitate the transfer of credits earned in diploma programs towards bachelor's degrees at universities, providing students with a seamless pathway to further education and career advancement.

3. The Association of Canadian Community Colleges (ACCC) represents 175 post-secondary institutions across Canada, which are officially known by a variety of titles. These titles include Community College, College, Technical Institute, University College, and Cégep. Each institution offers a diverse range of programs and services tailored to meet the needs of students and communities, contributing significantly to Canada's educational landscape and workforce development.

4. In Canada, both public and private post-secondary institutions may hold various designations such as "recognized," "authorized," "registered," or "licensed" by the government. These designations signify different levels of governmental oversight and approval. Public and private institutions that are "recognized" or "authorized" have been granted the authority to confer academic credentials by their provincial or territorial governments. This authority is typically established through charters or legislation, ensuring mechanisms for institutional and program quality assurance. These regulations aim to uphold standards of excellence and accountability across Canada's diverse higher education landscape.

Part time job and Other details

Part-Time Job

In Canada, international students have the opportunity to work both on and off campus while pursuing their studies, with or without a work permit. The Off-Campus Work Permit Program enables eligible foreign students to work off campus during their studies. To qualify, students must be enrolled full-time at a participating publicly funded post-secondary educational institution or in an approved program at an eligible privately funded institution. This program offers international students valuable opportunities to gain work experience and support themselves financially while completing their education in Canada.


Stay Back Options

International students studying in Canada for a minimum of an 8-month program are eligible to apply for a work permit under the Post-Graduation Work Permit Program (PGWPP). This program allows students to remain in Canada after completing their studies and gain valuable work experience. The duration of the stay-back period depends on the length of the program. If the study program is less than two years, the stay-back period will be equivalent to the duration of the course.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the intakes available in Canada?

The major intakes are Fall (September) and Spring (January) but many institutions have Summer intakes (May or June) as well.

Are Canadian degrees acknowledged by the global community?

Yes, Canadian degrees, Canadian Certificate and Diploma are acknowledged globally.

Can I get financial aid to study in Canada?

Yes, Canadian institutions offer financial aid in the form of scholarships, bursaries, on campus jobs, teaching assistantships, administrative assistantships, research assistantships, internships etc.

Does a student need a medical examination to study in Canada?

Yes medical examination is required for a student studying in Canada.

How much are the tuition fees in Canada for diploma program?

The tuition fees in Canada College should range between CAD$8,000 to 14,000 per annum, depending on the college and the program. This amount includes the health insurance also.

Can spouse work in Canada while a student is studying?

Spouse may apply for a Work Permit if the student studying full time at an authorized institution and have a Study Permit.

When is the deadline for receipt of supporting academic documents?

Deadline for receipt of supporting academic documents is 1 February 2014.

What is a Co-op program?

Co-operative (Co-op) programs are 4-16 week work placement programs where academically deserving students gain practical experience in the workplace and are paid by the employer. Students also get a chance to gain important work experience and to establish contacts with potential employers. Many graduates of co-op programs find full-time employment with their co-op employer.
